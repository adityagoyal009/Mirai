FAQ

Questions serious teams usually ask before trusting it

What do I need to provide to get a useful output?

A precise product description, buyer reality, why-now trigger, GTM shape, proof or traction, and the main unresolved risk. The `/submit` intake is structured around exactly those rating-critical facts.

Does Mirai replace real diligence?

No. It is a first-pass diligence and framing system. Its job is to sharpen the argument, surface risk, and make better follow-up work obvious.

Why use a model council instead of one strong model?

Because startup judgment is noisy. Independent scoring creates a better picture of where conviction is real and where confidence is just style.

What makes the swarm useful instead of gimmicky?

The swarm is role-based. It separates investor concerns from operator concerns, customer reactions, and contrarian objections, which is much closer to how real decisions get made.

Is the report instant?

No. Users sign in with Google, submit the company through `/submit`, and the request enters the reviewed queue before the report is delivered.

Does this expose the private dashboard?

No. The landing page, sign-in screen, and admin board are separate from the personal dashboard and warmboard surfaces.
